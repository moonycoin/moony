Branding Kit ​

Moony’s visual identity is intentionally simple, expressive, and open. This page defines the name, symbol, and usage conventions that shape how Moony appears across applications and interfaces. Each element reinforces Moony’s mission as a decentralized, accessible public asset.

The name “Moony” blends two ideas: moon and money. It channels the playful energy of cryptocurrency meme culture, where tokens “go to the moon", while signaling Moony’s purpose as digital cash. The name carries memetic and symbolic weight, rooted in a principled commitment to open, permissionless monetary infrastructure.

Icon Design ​

Moony’s symbol is minimal but rich in layered meaning. At first glance, it appears as a crescent moon rising over a horizon. On closer inspection, it encodes several ideas:

  • Moon above a horizon – Evokes natural cycles and long-term growth. The horizon line grounds the symbol, while the rising moon suggests upward motion and potential.
  • Binary logic – The shape subtly recalls 1s and 0s, reinforcing Moony’s identity as programmable digital money.
  • Trading notation – In technical charting, the symbol can be interpreted as "over" or "above," echoing Moony’s bonding curve and upward economic motion.

This icon is intentionally versatile, designed for clarity at small sizes, compatibility with light and dark themes, and easy use across interfaces.

Usage Guidelines ​

Moony can be represented with the ⍜ symbol (Unicode: U+235C “APL Functional Symbol Circle Star”) to denote token quantities, similar to how $ is used for U.S. dollars.

Examples ​

  • ⍜100 — one hundred Moony
  • ⍜0.5 — half a Moony
  • “Tip them ⍜2 for the post”
  • “The pool contains ⍜8,250”

Guidelines ​

  • Do not add a space between the symbol and the number (⍜100, not ⍜ 100)
  • Do not pluralize the unit (use ⍜100, not 100 moonies)
  • Use the symbol wherever concise value representation enhances clarity or UX

This convention reinforces Moony’s role as a usable unit of account and supports consistent communication across tools and interfaces.
